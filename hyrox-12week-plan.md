# Hyrox 12-Week Training Plan

**Beginner Tier:** 0 → First Finish (target: 120–150 min)  
**Intermediate Tier:** Sub-90 Minutes (target: 75–89 min)

---

## How to Use This Plan

Each week contains:
- **Run sessions** — specific pace zones and distances
- **Strength/skill sessions** — Hyrox station simulation and drills
- **Mobility & recovery** — prescribed active recovery and stretching

### Pace Zones

| Zone | Effort | Perceived Exertion (RPE) | Talk Test |
|------|--------|--------------------------|-----------|
| Z1 | Easy recovery jog | 2–3/10 | Full conversation |
| Z2 | Steady aerobic | 4–5/10 | Short sentences |
| Z3 | Moderate tempo | 6–7/10 | 3–4 words at a time |
| Z4 | Threshold / hard | 8–9/10 | Single words |
| Z5 | Sprint / max effort | 10/10 | None |

### Weekly Schedule Template

| Day | Beginner | Intermediate |
|-----|----------|--------------|
| Mon | Run (Z2) | Run (Z3/Z4 intervals) |
| Tue | Strength/Skill | Strength/Skill |
| Wed | Active Recovery or Off | Run (Z2) |
| Thu | Strength/Skill | Strength/Skill |
| Fri | Run (Z1) or Off | Run (Z2 easy) |
| Sat | Long Run + Station Preview | Long Run + Hyrox Simulation |
| Sun | Mobility / Foam Rolling | Mobility / Foam Rolling |

## Week 1 — Foundation & Assessment

### Beginner

**Monday — Run: Aerobic Base**
- 20 min easy jog (Z1–Z2)
- Focus: cadence ~170–180 steps/min, nose breathing
- Cool down: 5 min walk

**Tuesday — Strength/Skill: Technique**
- **Sled Push Drills:** 3 × 20 m light sled (about 50% race weight), focus on torso angle (~45°), short choppy steps
- **Wallball Technique:** 3 × 15 reps at 6 kg (women) / 9 kg (men), focus on full squat depth, ball to 10 ft target line
- **Burpee Broad Jump:** 3 × 5 reps — step burpee (no jump-back), focus on soft landing
- Cool down: 10 min light stretching

**Wednesday — Active Recovery**
- 20–30 min walk or light bike
- Foam roll: quads, hamstrings, glutes, calves — 2 min per muscle group

**Thursday — Strength/Skill: Engine Builder**
- **Rower:** 3 × 500 m, 2 min rest between intervals — aim for 2:15–2:30/500 m split (beginner) or 1:55–2:05/500 m (intermediate)
- **Kettlebell Swings:** 3 × 15 reps at 16 kg / 24 kg
- **Lunges:** 3 × 10 per leg (bodyweight)
- Cool down: 10 min stretching

**Friday — Rest or Easy Walk**

**Saturday — Long Run + Introduction**
- **Run:** 25 min at Z1–Z2
- **Intro:** Practice 1 round of the Hyrox stations at easy effort — Farmer's Carry 50 m, Sled Push 25 m, Sled Pull 25 m, Burpee Broad Jump 10 reps
- Cool down: 5 min walk + full body stretch

**Sunday — Mobility**
- **Hip Mobility Flow:** 10 min — world's greatest stretch, 90/90 hip switch, couch stretch
- **Ankle Mobility:** 5 min — dorsiflexion banded distraction, calf stretches
- **Thoracic Spine:** 5 min — foam roller openers, cat-cow, thread-the-needle

### Intermediate

**Monday — Run: Threshold Intervals**
- Warm-up: 10 min easy jog
- 5 × 3 min at Z4 (threshold pace), 2 min easy jog recovery
- Cool down: 10 min easy jog

**Tuesday — Strength/Skill: Station Simulation**
- **Sled Push:** 5 × 20 m at ~70% race weight, short rest (45 sec)
- **Wallball:** 3 × 30 reps at 9 kg (women) / 12 kg (men), EMOM style
- **Burpee Broad Jump:** 3 × 8 reps (full burpee with jump-back), focus on explosive broad jump
- **Farmer's Carry:** 3 × 50 m at 24 kg per hand / 32 kg per hand
- Cool down: 10 min stretching

**Wednesday — Run: Aerobic Volume**
- 35 min steady state at Z2
- Focus: maintain cadence 175–185 spm

**Thursday — Strength/Skill: Engine Builder**
- **Rower:** 4 × 500 m at 1:50–1:55 split, 90 sec rest
- **SkiErg (or sub):** 3 × 300 m at high effort, 60 sec rest
- **Kettlebell Swings:** 4 × 15 reps at 24 kg / 32 kg
- Cool down: 10 min stretching

**Friday — Recovery Run**
- 20 min very easy (Z1), focus on form
- Optional: 15 min foam rolling afterward

**Saturday — Long Run + Hyrox Simulation**
- **Run:** 40 min at Z2
- **Simulation:** 1 full round of Hyrox — 1 km run, then all 8 stations at moderate effort, then 1 km run
- Cool down: 5 min walk

**Sunday — Mobility**
- 20 min full mobility session (same as Beginner but extended)
- Add: Pigeon pose (2 min per leg), happy baby, seated hamstring stretch

## Skill Drill Library

### Sled Push — Key Cues

1. **Torso angle:** ~45° to ground — don't stand upright
2. **Stride:** Short, choppy steps — not long strides
3. **Arm drive:** Pump arms aggressively to generate power
4. **Keep the bar:** Hands on the uprights, not the crossbar
5. **Practice drill:** 5 × 15 m with light weight, focus only on form

**Drill progression:**
1. Empty sled — 3 × 20 m (technique only)
2. 50% race weight — 3 × 20 m (add speed)
3. 75% race weight — 4 × 20 m (add fatigue management)
4. Race weight — 4 × 30 m (full simulation)

### Wallball — Key Cues

1. **Squat depth:** Hip crease below knee
2. **Target height:** 10 ft (3 m) for men, 9 ft (2.7 m) for women
3. **Breathing:** Exhale on the throw, inhale on the catch
4. **Catch position:** Soft hands at chest height — don't let ball drop to waist
5. **Foot position:** Slightly wider than shoulder-width, toes slightly out

**Drill progression:**
1. Air squats — 3 × 15 (establish depth)
2. Squat + press (no ball) — 3 × 10
3. Light ball (6 kg) — 3 × 15 (build rhythm)
4. Race weight — 3 × 20 (add volume)
5. Race weight — 100 reps for time (simulation)

### Burpee Broad Jump — Key Cues

1. **Burpee:** Chest touches ground, hips fully extend
2. **Jump:** Two-foot takeoff, land soft and balanced
3. **Distance per rep:** Aim for ~1 m per rep (80 m total)
4. **Breathing:** Inhale on burpee down, exhale on jump
5. **Pacing:** Steady rhythm — don't sprint the first 20 m

**Drill progression:**
1. Step burpee (no jump-back) + small hop — 3 × 5
2. Full burpee + step broad jump — 3 × 8
3. Full burpee + full broad jump — 4 × 10
4. 80 m for time (simulation of race distance)

## Mobility & Recovery Guide

### Daily Mobility (5–10 min)

| Exercise | Duration | When |
|----------|----------|------|
| World's Greatest Stretch | 1 min per side | Morning / Pre-run |
| Couch Stretch | 2 min per side | Post-run |
| 90/90 Hip Switch | 1 min per side | Any time |
| Cat-Cow | 1 min | Pre-workout |
| Ankle Dorsiflexion | 1 min per side | Pre-run |
| Thoracic Spine Rotation | 1 min per side | Pre-strength |

### Post-Workout Recovery

1. **Immediate:** 5 min walk to cool down
2. **15 min post:** Carbohydrate + protein (30–40 g + 20–30 g)
3. **30 min post:** Full body stretch (5–10 min)
4. **Evening:** Foam roll any sore areas (2 min per muscle group)

### Weekly Recovery Schedule

- **After hard sessions:** 15 min foam rolling
- **Active recovery days:** Walk, bike, swim — low impact
- **Deload weeks (4, 7):** Massage or sauna session if available

### Nutrition Notes for Hyrox

- **Pre-race (2–3 hours):** Simple carbs — oatmeal, banana, toast with honey
- **During race (per 30 min):** 30–60 g carbs — gels, chews, or sports drink
- **Post-race (within 1 hour):** Carbs + protein to replenish glycogen
- **Hydration:** Aim for 500–750 ml per hour during training; practice race-day hydration strategy during Week 9–10 long sessions

## Race Day Pacing Strategy

**The single biggest mistake in Hyrox:** Going too hard on the first 1 km run. Your first km should feel embarrassingly easy. If you're breathing hard after km 1, you went too fast. Trust the taper, trust the training, and hold pace on run 1.

**Station transition rule:** Move immediately to the next station when you finish. Don't stand and breathe — walk while recovering. Every second you stand still is a second you can't get back.

**The wallball wall:** Around rep 60–70 of wallball, most people hit a mental wall. Break it into chunks — "just get to 75" — then "just get to 85" — then "10 more." If you've practiced sets of 20+ in training, you can do 20 more on race day.
